* **June 1, 1892**: Joseph Hopper born in Stanford, Kentucky to George Dunlap Hopper and Katherine Elizabeth Higgens Hopper.[^joseph-bio]
* **1914**: Joseph Hopper graduates magna cum laude from Centre College.[^memoir]
* **1917**: Joseph Hopper graduates from Louisville Presbyterian Theological Seminary with Bachelor of Divinity.[^joseph-bio]
* **April 24, 1917**: Joseph Hopper licensed to preach by Transylvania Presbytery of the PCUS.[^joseph-bio]
* **October 9, 1917**: Joseph Hopper ordained by West Lexington Presbytery.[^joseph-bio]
* **December 18, 1919**: Joseph Hopper marries Annis Barron of Rock Hill, South Carolina.[^memoir]
* **February 1920**: Joseph and Annis Hopper depart for Korea as PCUS missionaries, assigned to Mokpo.[^memoir]
* **May 17, 1921**: Joseph Barron Hopper born in Kwangju, Korea.[^joe-b-bio]
* **April 1920**: Joseph Hopper preaches his first sermon to Korean congregation at Kwangju leper church.[^memoir]
* **1928**: Joseph Hopper earns ThM from Union Presbyterian Seminary.[^joseph-bio]
* **1931**: Joseph Hopper teaches at Presbyterian Theological Seminary in Pyongyang; Joe B. attends fifth grade at Pyongyang Foreign School.[^pyengyang]
* **1934**: Joe B. begins high school at Pyongyang Foreign School.[^pyengyang]
* **Summer 1935**: Hopper family takes furlough trip around the world via Japan, Singapore, Palestine, and England.[^trip]
* **June 7, 1938**: Joe B. graduates from Pyongyang Foreign School as valedictorian.[^pyengyang]
* **1939**: Joseph Hopper earns ThD from Union Presbyterian Seminary.[^joseph-bio]
* **1939**: Joe B. received under care as candidate for ministry by Concord Presbytery of the PCUS.[^joe-b-bio]
* **1940**: Pyongyang Foreign School closes; most missionaries evacuate Korea before Pearl Harbor.[^pyengyang]
* **1941-1945**: Joseph Hopper serves as stated supply at Emory Church in Decatur, Georgia (1941-1942) and Royal Oak Church in Marion, Virginia (1942-1945) during wartime evacuation.[^joseph-bio]
* **1942**: Joe B. graduates magna cum laude from Davidson College; member of Phi Beta Kappa.[^joe-b-bio]
* **July 19, 1945**: Joe B. Hopper marries Dorothy "Dot" Longenecker at Blackstone Presbyterian Church in Virginia.[^dorothy]
* **August 12, 1945**: Joe B. Hopper ordained by Montgomery Presbytery of the PCUS.[^joe-b-bio]
* **1945**: Joe B. graduates from Union Presbyterian Seminary with Bachelor of Divinity.[^joe-b-bio]
* **1945-1947**: Joe B. serves as pastor of Blackwater and Piedmont Presbyterian Churches in Callaway, Virginia.[^joe-b-bio]
* **Summer 1946**: Joe B. and Dot commissioned as missionaries to Korea at Montreat Missions Conference.[^dorothy]
* **1947-1948**: Joe B. and Dot attend Yale Institute of Far Eastern Languages while awaiting permission to enter Korea.[^dorothy]
* **1948**: Joe B. and Dot arrive in Korea; Joseph and Annis Hopper on dock to greet them.[^dorothy]
* **December 23, 1948**: Barron Hopper born in Chonju, delivered by Dr. Paul Crane.[^dorothy]
* **June 25, 1950**: Korean War begins; missionaries evacuate from Chonju following midnight phone call to American Embassy.[^dorothy]
* **1950**: Joe B. and Dot evacuate to Japan then Seattle; David Hopper born in Richmond two months after evacuation.[^dorothy]
* **1950**: Joe B. earns ThM from Union Presbyterian Seminary.[^joe-b-bio]
* **September 1951**: Joe B. returns alone to Korea to replace Dr. W.A. Linton in Chonju; Dot and children remain in Quitman, Georgia with her parents for 19 months.[^dorothy]
* **Easter 1954**: Dot and children reunited with Joe B. in Korea after families permitted to re-enter.[^dorothy]
* **1954**: Margaret Lois Hopper born in Chonju, delivered by Dr. Paul Crane.[^dorothy]
* **1956**: Joseph Hopper retires from Korea after 36 years of missionary service.[^joseph-bio]
* **1965**: Joe B. receives Doctor of Divinity degree from Davidson College.[^joe-b-bio]
* **February 20, 1971**: Joseph Hopper dies.[^joseph-bio]
* **1986**: Joe B. and Dot retire from Korea after 38 years of missionary service.[^joe-b-bio]
* **April 27, 1992**: Joe B. Hopper dies in Montreat, North Carolina.[^joe-b-bio]
* **December 4, 2015**: Dorothy Longenecker Hopper dies in Hillsborough, North Carolina.[^dot-bio]
